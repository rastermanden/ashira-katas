3rd Kyu

三級

Kata required for this level

初心者の形一

Beginner Form 1

Shoshinsha no Kata Sono Ichi

10 steps →

Shoshinsha no Kata Sono Ichi is the first kata of Ashihara Karate. Emphasis is placed on stepping back, judging distance and nagashi type parrying. The kata introduces the fundamental attack-defense relationship using basic punches and front kicks.

基本形一

Basic Form 1

Kihon no Kata Sono Ichi

10 steps →

Kihon no Kata Sono Ichi emphasises stepping out to the side (hiki ashi and side step). The concepts of Irimi (entering) and Kyushu (absorbing) are introduced. Techniques build on the Shoshinsha kata, now incorporating more dynamic counters.

組手の形一

Sparring Form 1

Kumite no Kata Sono Ichi

10 steps →

Kumite no Kata Sono Ichi is the first of the sparring forms in Ashihara Karate. The kata emphasises the use of the shin block (sune-uke) against roundhouse kicks followed by close-range counters, as well as the mawashi-uke (wrap block) entry to knee strikes and throwing techniques.

初心者の形二

Beginner Form 2

Shoshinsha no Kata Sono Ni

10 steps →

Shoshinsha no Kata Sono Ni builds on the first beginner form by introducing combination counters including hook and uppercut punches. Front kicks are now used as the primary attack, and the defender responds with multi-technique counter sequences.

初心者の形三

Beginner Form 3

Shoshinsha no Kata Sono San

10 steps →

Shoshinsha no Kata Sono San introduces side kicks and more advanced roundhouse kick combinations into the defender repertoire. The kata develops the ability to handle kicks from multiple angles and adds entries with kansetsu-geri (joint kick) targeting the supporting leg.

基本形二

Basic Form 2

Kihon no Kata Sono Ni

10 steps →

Kihon no Kata Sono Ni builds on the first basic form by introducing yoko-geri (side kick) and further expanding counter combinations. The kata develops the use of the foot switch and close-range combination follow-ups after blocking.

組手の形二

Sparring Form 2

Kumite no Kata Sono Ni

10 steps →

Kumite no Kata Sono Ni builds on the first sparring form by adding extended counter sequences including double knee strikes and spinning back kicks. The kata develops flow and continuity after the initial defensive response.

投げの形二

Throwing Form 2

Nage no Kata Sono Ni

10 steps →

Nage no Kata Sono Ni builds on the first throwing form by adding knee strikes before each throw. Against roundhouse kicks, a hiza-geri is inserted before the maki komi nage; against front kicks, a knee strike to the back of the head precedes the ura-nage. This develops the principle of weakening the opponent before throwing.

基本形三

Basic Form 3

Kihon no Kata Sono San

10 steps →

Kihon no Kata Sono San introduces advanced kick attacks including jodan ushiro mawashi-geri (upper back roundhouse kick), ushiro-geri (back kick) and san kaku-geri (triangle kick). The kata develops kyushu-step entries against kicks and extends counter sequences with ushiro mawari tettsui (spinning hammer fist).

投げの形三

Throwing Form 3

Nage no Kata Sono San

10 steps →

Nage no Kata Sono San introduces hikitaoshi (take-down/throw-down) as an alternative to the ura-nage, and incorporates multi-kick counters before the throw. Step 3 and 8 both feature complex entry sequences using back-position approaches before the final hikitaoshi.

実戦の形一

Real Fight Form 1

Jissen no Kata Sono Ichi

10 steps →

Jissen no Kata Sono Ichi emphasises close-range fighting with inside (irimi) blocks and immediate elbow or punch counters. The kata develops rapid combinations after entering inside the opponent's guard, incorporating shin/elbow blocks (hiji/sune-uke) against kicks and finishing with maki komi nage or hikikuzushi.

組手の形三

Sparring Form 3

Kumite no Kata Sono San

10 steps →

Kumite no Kata Sono San introduces stopping techniques against low kicks and combination attacks (mae-geri + tsuki). Throwing techniques such as ura-nage (back throw) appear prominently, and the kata develops the use of pivot-leg (jikuashi) counters.
